Subject Program

This Year 9 Metalwork program guides you through the safe and creative process of designing and constructing two sheet‑metal projects: a toolbox and a fishing rod holder. You’ll learn about workshop safety, risk management, metals and their properties, marking out and cutting techniques, joining methods and fabrication processes. Each week builds practical skills and theoretical understanding, culminating in a finished product you can be proud of.

Syllabus

The program aligns with the Stage 5 Industrial Technology – Metal course outcomes. Students will develop knowledge of metals, processes and associated technologies and apply the design process to create functional products. Learning outcomes include WHS compliance, design and communication skills, correct use of hand and power tools, understanding material properties, and evaluation of work practices.

Course Outcomes

  • IND5‑1: Identify, assess, apply and manage the risks and WHS issues associated with using a range of tools, equipment, materials, processes and technologies.
  • IND5‑2: Apply design principles in the modification, development and production of projects.
  • IND5‑3: Identify, select and use a range of hand and machine tools, equipment and processes to produce quality practical projects.
  • IND5‑4: Select, justify and use a range of relevant materials for specific applications.
  • IND5‑5: Select, interpret and apply a range of suitable communication techniques in the development, planning, production and presentation of ideas and projects.
  • IND5‑6: Identify and participate in collaborative work practices in the learning environment.
  • IND5‑7: Apply and transfer skills, processes and materials to a variety of contexts and projects.
  • IND5‑8: Evaluate products in terms of functional, economic, aesthetic and environmental qualities and quality of construction.
  • IND5‑9: Describe, analyse and use a range of current, new and emerging technologies and their various applications.
  • IND5‑10: Describe, analyse and evaluate the impact of technology on society, the environment and cultural issues locally and globally.

Scope & Sequence

The scope and sequence below outlines the focus of each unit along with the key outcomes addressed.

Unit Topic Key Outcomes Addressed
1 Workshop Safety & First Aid IND5‑1, IND5‑6 – develop safe workshop practices, manage risks, use PPE and demonstrate basic first aid procedures.
2 WHS Legislation & Risk Management IND5‑1, IND5‑10 – understand WHS laws, apply risk management techniques, complete safety tests and analyse the impact of regulation.
3 Materials – Ferrous & Non‑Ferrous Metals IND5‑4, IND5‑7, IND5‑9 – investigate metal properties and uses, select appropriate materials, and explore heat‑treatment processes.
4 Marking‑out & Measuring Tools IND5‑3, IND5‑5 – use measuring and marking tools accurately, interpret drawings and communicate measurements clearly.
5 Cutting & Shaping Techniques IND5‑3, IND5‑4, IND5‑7 – perform shearing, cutting and forming operations safely on sheet and bar stock.
6 Joining Techniques IND5‑3, IND5‑4, IND5‑8 – join components using fasteners, rivets, soldering and welding; evaluate joints for strength and quality.
7 Lathe & Machining Operations IND5‑3, IND5‑8, IND5‑9 – operate lathes and other machines to perform facing, turning, drilling, knurling and thread cutting.
8 Heat Treatment & Finishing IND5‑4, IND5‑8, IND5‑9 – apply annealing, quenching, tempering and finishing processes such as buffing, powder coating and painting.
9 CNC & Advanced Manufacturing IND5‑2, IND5‑9, IND5‑10 – explore CNC, laser cutting, waterjet and 3D printing; evaluate technological advantages and impacts.
10 Design & Project Planning IND5‑2, IND5‑5, IND5‑7, IND5‑8 – apply the design process to plan and document projects, produce sketches, cutting lists and time plans, and evaluate outcomes.

Assessments

Assessment tasks include theory quizzes for each unit, a project management portfolio containing sketches, cutting lists and time plans, and a practical component where students fabricate the toolbox and fishing rod holder. Regular peer and self‑assessments will help you reflect on your work and plan improvements.

At the end of the course you will sit a comprehensive End‑of‑Year Exam that revisits all of the units studied. The exam is delivered online and is protected by a password for integrity. Your teacher will provide the password when it is time to sit the test. You can access the exam here: Start the End‑of‑Year Exam

Project Unit

The major project combines sheet metal fabrication and design thinking. You’ll plan and construct a toolbox using sheet‑metal folding, riveting and finishing techniques. Later in the term you’ll build a fishing rod holder, applying lathe work and welding to create a sturdy ground spike and rod cradle. Through these projects you’ll document your research, sketches, materials lists and evaluations.
