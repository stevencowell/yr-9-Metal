Week 12: Staining & Varnishing Techniques

Introduction

This week focuses on applying colour and protection to your Carry-all. You’ll learn the difference between stains and varnishes, how to choose the right products, and best practices for application.

Staining Basics

  • Stain penetrates wood fibres, enhancing grain and adding colour.
  • Types: Oil-based, water-based, gel stains.
  • Always stir—don’t shake—to avoid bubbles.
  • Apply with brush or cloth and wipe off excess to control depth.

Varnishing & Clear Coats

  • Varnish sits on the surface, providing durable, protective finish.
  • Options: Polyurethane (oil- or water-based), spar varnish, lacquer.
  • Apply thin coats with smooth brush or foam applicator.
  • Sand lightly (320–400 grit) between coats for adhesion.

Surface Preparation & Safety

  • Ensure surface is clean, dry, and dust-free.
  • Test on scrap to check colour and compatibility.
  • Work in well-ventilated area; wear gloves and respirator as needed.

Discussion Starters

  • What stain colour would best suit your project and why?
  • How many varnish coats do you plan to apply, and why?
  • What safety measures are important when using solvent-based finishes?
