Unit 1: Workshop Safety & First Aid

Safe Workshop Practices

A safe workshop begins with your attitude. Always follow signage, return leftover steel to the racks and keep benches clear of off‑cuts. Wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) such as safety glasses, protective footwear and clothing. When heating metal, wear leather gloves to protect against burns. Work collaboratively, be aware of others and never use machines when tired or distracted.

Hierarchy of Control & PPE

The hierarchy of controls describes methods for eliminating or reducing hazards. In order of effectiveness these are: elimination (remove the hazard), substitution (replace with something safer), engineering controls (isolate people from the hazard), administrative controls (change the way people work) and finally PPE. PPE is the last line of defence and should only be relied upon when other controls are not practical. For example, fit guards to machines (engineering control) and use PPE such as eye protection and hearing protection as a backup.

First Aid & Incident Reporting

Accidents can still happen. Know the location of first aid kits and follow your teacher’s instructions in an emergency. First aid procedures provide immediate assistance to someone injured or ill. Report all incidents and hazards to your teacher or supervisor so risks can be addressed. Complete mandatory online safety tests (such as Onguard) before starting practical work to ensure you understand safety rules and procedures.
