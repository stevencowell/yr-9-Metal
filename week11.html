Week 11: Handles, Hardware & Functional Design

Introduction

Now that the main structure of your Carry-all is complete, it's time to focus on hardware elements — especially handles, hinges, and functional features. These final additions may seem small, but they have a big impact on how your project looks, feels, and works.

Types of Handles

  • Integrated timber handle – part of the side panels or shaped into the top rail
  • Dowel handle – a round rod fixed through or across the top
  • Rope handle – threaded through holes and knotted inside
  • Metal hardware handle – pre-made and screwed in place

Good handle placement: Centres the weight when carrying, feels comfortable in the hand, and avoids blocking access to tools inside.

Attaching Hardware Safely

Whether you're adding hinges for a lid, metal handles, or corner braces, you need to mark carefully, pre-drill small pilot holes, and screw in gently. Pilot holes help prevent timber from splitting, screws from going in crooked, and surface damage or blowout. Use a bradawl or punch to start the hole before drilling, and always test operation of moving parts before final tightening.

Improving Functionality

  • Add tool racks inside
  • Include dividers for sections
  • Attach rubber feet underneath
  • Shape the base to stop it tipping

Discussion Starters

  • Which handle style fits your project best?
  • What did you learn about pilot holes and why they matter?
  • What small improvements could make your Carry-all more user-friendly?
