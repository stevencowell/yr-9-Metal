Unit 7: Lathe & Machining Operations

Lathe Basics

A metal lathe spins the workpiece while a cutting tool removes material. The major parts include the headstock (holds the spindle and chuck), tailstock (supports the opposite end using a centre or drill chuck) and carriage (carries the cutting tool and moves along the bed). Always remove the chuck key immediately after tightening the chuck, and keep loose clothing and hair away from rotating parts.

Common Operations

  • Facing – produces a flat surface on the end of a workpiece by moving the cutting tool perpendicular to the axis.
  • Parallel (turning) – reduces the diameter of the workpiece by feeding the tool along the axis.
  • Drilling – uses a drill bit in the tailstock to create holes along the centre line.
  • Knurling – presses a patterned roller into the surface to create a textured grip.
  • Thread cutting – cuts internal or external screw threads using taps (internal) or dies (external) or by single‑point threading on the lathe.

Other Machines

Drilling machines, milling machines and cold saws expand your ability to machine metal. A drill press holds the drill perpendicular to the work and allows you to control feed rate. A milling machine removes material using a rotating cutter; it can cut slots, pockets or complex shapes. Always clamp work securely and choose appropriate speeds for the material and cutter.

Safety Reminders

Wear eye protection, tie back long hair and avoid loose sleeves. Check that tools are sharp and workpieces are securely clamped. Do not touch chips or swarf with bare hands — use a brush or hook to remove them. Pay attention to feed speeds: high speeds produce heat and can damage tools; too slow can cause chatter. Ask your teacher before making adjustments.
