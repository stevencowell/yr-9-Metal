Unit 9: CNC & Advanced Manufacturing

Computer Numerical Control (CNC)

CNC machines use computer programs to control the movement of tools and workpieces. They offer excellent repeatability and precision compared to manual machining. Typical CNC equipment includes mills, lathes and routers. Before machining, a CAD file is translated into G‑code that tells the machine where to move and how fast. Digital design reduces waste and ensures accuracy.

Laser Cutting

Laser cutting is a high‑precision CNC process that uses a focused, high‑power laser to cut or melt material. It’s excellent for one‑off jobs and low‑to‑medium volume production. The laser can cut steel, aluminium and other metals with a clean edge and minimal distortion. Never look directly at the laser beam; follow safety procedures and keep the work area enclosed.

Waterjet & Plasma Cutting

Waterjet cutting uses a high‑pressure jet of water mixed with abrasive to cut through metal, stone and composites. It produces no heat‑affected zone, making it ideal for materials that could warp. Plasma cutting uses an electrically conductive gas arc to slice through metal; it’s fast and effective for thicker sections.

Rapid Prototyping

Rapid prototyping technologies, such as 3D printing (additive manufacturing), allow you to create models and functional parts directly from digital files. They are invaluable for testing designs before committing to full‑scale production. Understanding these advanced processes prepares you for the evolving manufacturing landscape.
