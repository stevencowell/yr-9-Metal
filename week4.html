Unit 4: Marking‑out & Measuring Tools

Marking‑out Tools

Accurate layout is the foundation of quality metalwork. Use the right tool for each marking task:

  • Engineer’s square – draws and checks 90° (right‑angle) lines on material.
  • Scriber – scratches fine, permanent lines on metal; must be harder than the material being marked.
  • Odd‑leg (jenny) calipers – have one pointed leg and one bent leg for drawing parallel lines along an edge.
  • Marking‑out blue – a dye applied to the surface so scribed lines stand out clearly.
  • Dot punch & centre punch – place small witness marks and a dimple at the centre of a hole to guide drill bits.
  • Dividers – mark circles and equal divisions; adjust to the required radius.

Measuring Tools

Measurement accuracy prevents wasted material. A steel rule gives quick, straight measurements; tapes are useful for longer lengths. Vernier callipers measure inside, outside and depth dimensions with precision. When transferring measurements, use a marking gauge or combination square to ensure lines are parallel and square. Always double‑check your measurements before cutting — “measure twice, cut once.”

Good Practice

Keep scribing tools sharp and store them safely. Apply even pressure when scribing; a light, single pass leaves a clean line. Use the dot punch before drilling to prevent the drill bit from wandering. Wipe off marking blue once work is finished so it doesn’t obscure surface defects.
