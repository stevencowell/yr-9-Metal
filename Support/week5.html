Task 5: Joinery & Fasteners

Support Theory

Joining pieces of metal can be done mechanically or by fusion. Mechanical methods include screws, bolts and rivets, which clamp materials together using threads or deformation. Soldering and brazing melt a low‑melting filler metal to bond parts without melting the parent metals. Threads tapped into holes allow screws or bolts to engage securely. Gas Metal Arc Welding (GMAW) uses an electric arc and a shielding gas to fuse metals with a continuously fed wire.
