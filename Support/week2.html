Task 2: Risk Assessment Matrix

Support Theory

A risk matrix helps prioritise hazards by considering how likely an incident is and how severe the consequences could be. After identifying a hazard, assess its likelihood (chance of occurring) and consequence (how serious the outcome). A high likelihood and severe consequence means a high‑priority risk requiring strong control measures, such as elimination or substitution.
