Task 1: Hazard Identification

Support Theory

Identifying hazards is the first step in keeping a workshop safe. A hazard is anything with the potential to cause harm: sharp offcuts left on benches, spills on the floor, frayed cords or loose clothing near machinery. When you recognise a hazard, report it and help remove or control it before beginning work. This reduces the likelihood of accidents and injuries.
