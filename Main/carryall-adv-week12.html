Advanced Theory: Assembly Scenario

CAM software converts geometry into toolpaths. Adaptive clearing maintains constant cutter engagement, lowering tool wear. Feed rate (F) combines chip‑load and spindle speed (S). Post‑processors output machine‑specific G/M codes; modal commands persist until changed. Lead‑in arcs (G3) reduce cutter shock. Tool length offset (G43) relies on accurate touch‑off probing. High‑speed machining uses look‑ahead controllers to maintain corner quality at >10 m min⁻¹.
