Unit 10: Design & Project Planning

Design Process

Every project begins with a design brief that outlines the problem you are trying to solve. Conduct research, brainstorm ideas and create concept sketches. Narrow your concepts and develop detailed working drawings that show accurate dimensions and assembly methods. Consider aesthetics, function and how the product will be used.

Materials & Costing

Choose materials based on their properties (strength, corrosion resistance, weight), availability and cost. Prepare a cutting list that itemises each part, its dimensions and material. Use a spreadsheet to estimate quantities and total costs. Don’t forget consumables like rivets, bolts and finishing supplies.

Time Plans & Project Management

A time plan breaks the project into stages and allocates time to each task. This helps you manage workload and meet deadlines. Include milestones such as completing sketches, cutting parts, assembly and finishing. Adjust your plan if tasks take longer than expected.

Evaluation & Communication

Throughout the project, document your progress with photos and notes. After completing the product, evaluate it against your design brief: does it meet the requirements? What could be improved? Peer and self‑assessment helps you refine your skills. Communicate your ideas clearly — using CAD drawings, models and presentations — to share your design with others.
