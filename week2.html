Unit 2: WHS Legislation & Risk Management

Work Health & Safety Legislation

Work Health and Safety (WHS) laws outline the responsibilities of employers (known as persons conducting a business or undertaking) and workers to ensure a safe workplace. Schools must provide safe equipment, training and supervision, while students must follow instructions, use equipment correctly and report hazards. Many tasks require a Safe Work Method Statement (SWMS) outlining hazards, control measures and PPE requirements. Always read and follow these documents when using machines such as the Magnabend or lathe.

Risk Management Process

Risk management involves identifying hazards, assessing the likelihood and consequence of harm using a risk matrix, and implementing control measures. The hierarchy of controls places elimination at the top, followed by substitution, engineering controls, administrative controls and finally personal protective equipment. Always aim to eliminate or substitute hazards before relying on PPE. For example, remove sharp off‑cuts from your workspace rather than simply wearing gloves.

Safe Work Method Statements & Signage

Machines and processes often have posted signage indicating speed settings, emergency stop buttons and required PPE. A Safe Work Method Statement (SWMS) describes the correct setup, operation and control measures for a specific task. Review these before starting work. If in doubt, ask your teacher to explain procedures and demonstrate safe operation. Reporting hazards and near misses is a responsibility shared by everyone.
